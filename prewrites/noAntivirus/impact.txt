With Defender inactive, any form of malware, including command and control (C2) agents, can be introduced and run on the system without being detected or stopped. In the current case, /team was able to deploy C2 malware and gain persistent remote access, allowing for covert control, data exfiltration, or additional attacks such as credential harvesting. If a single endpoint remains unprotected in a managed environment, adversaries can exploit it as a foothold to compromise broader network resources, disrupt services, steal sensitive information, or propagate ransomware. The absence of endpoint protection erodes the organization's defense in depth strategy, making containment and detection significantly more difficult.
