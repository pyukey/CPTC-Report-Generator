Windows Defender is the integrated antivirus and anti-malware solution provided by Microsoft for Windows operating systems. On multiple workstations, /team discovered that Windows Defender protection was disabled. As the default line of defense, Defender provides real-time monitoring and helps detect and mitigate viruses, trojans, ransomware, and other forms of malicious software. Disabling it removes a foundational security layer, exposing machines to a wide range of threats that would otherwise be blocked or quarantined automatically by Defender. Malware authors and attackers commonly target built-in security by turning off protections, rendering systems unable to defend against known and emerging risks. This is particularly concerning in enterprise networks, where such weaknesses can be exploited to launch further attacks or penetrate critical systems.
